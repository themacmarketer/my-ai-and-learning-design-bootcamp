### Design Decisions

**Why 4 personas, not 5:**
The brief identifies three functional areas (events, social media, branding/comms) and three seniority tiers (executive, manager, director). The audience research shows relatively homogeneous GA4 baselines across the cohort (None–Remember for all). Primary differentiators are function, seniority, motivation, and friction type — not prior GA4 knowledge. Four personas bracket these axes without requiring fabricated within-tier differentiation (R12). A 5th persona can be added if the user identifies an underrepresented segment post-review.

**Why not a "self-taught analytics enthusiast" persona:**
Audience research S2 estimates GA4 starting level at Remember (at best) for all participants. There is no evidence in the inputs of any participant with significant UA or advanced analytics experience. WOGAA-based misconceptions (the most plausible source of false confidence) are embedded in Priya's profile instead. Adding an enthusiast persona would require fabricating an audience segment not supported by the research.

**Acknowledged limitation:**
No pre-workshop survey data exists. All Bloom's level assignments are derived from role descriptions and prior tool usage patterns — not from direct participant assessment. The weakest assumption is Wei Jie's social analytics baseline (see Aspirational Check). A pre-workshop questionnaire (recommended in Section 7) would convert these hypotheses into confirmed profiles.

## Section 4: Note on Persona Limitations (R12)

These personas are hypotheses derived from research, not confirmed profiles. All downstream design decisions should be treated as provisional until validated with real learners. Specific uncertainties:

1. **No pre-workshop survey data** — actual GA4 usage levels are unknown (audience research gap #1). Bloom's level assignments are derived from role descriptions and documented tool usage patterns, not direct participant assessment.
2. **WOGAA proficiency assumed but unconfirmed** — if participants do not actively use WOGAA either, the "false confidence" friction in Priya may not apply (audience research gap #2).
3. **Team composition unknown** — whether participants come from one team or multiple teams affects group dynamics, especially Darren's social effect on the room (audience research gap #5).
4. **Wei Jie's social analytics baseline is the weakest assumption** in this document. It is the only aspirationally-rated attribute, and it has a material downstream design impact. Confirm with pre-workshop questionnaire before finalising.
5. **Participant headcount by function/seniority is unconfirmed** — percentage estimates in each persona's reasoning section are approximations from the "20–30 staff, executives to directors" brief description. Actual distribution may differ.

### 6.2 Design Implications of Cross-Persona Dynamics

**Highest-priority mitigation:** Darren's engagement must be secured in the first 30 minutes. If he mentally checks out early, the social dynamics of the room shift against the facilitator. This is the single highest-stakes design decision the curriculum faces.
