## Limitations (R7)

1. All friction scores and differentiation responses are hypotheses derived from synthetic walk-throughs, not observed learner behaviour.
2. Green scores may underreport friction — AI assessment structurally favours positive signals (NN/g, 2024).
3. Facilitation cues (Response type d) depend on facilitator skill and willingness — particularly cues requiring push-back on a director (Darren M9).
4. Some responses create dependencies on each other: if upstream scaffolds are omitted (e.g., M2 vocabulary bridges not distributed), downstream responses (e.g., M6 vocabulary refresh) may be insufficient.
5. Cross-persona social dynamics (Darren's presence affecting Siti's engagement) are not modelled — each persona is treated independently.
