## Chain-of-Thought: Objective Derivation Process

**Evidence drawn on:**
- Brief's business objective: "help staff understand and use GA4 more effectively alongside existing GovTech measurement platforms like WOGAA to measure business performance"
- Persona success criteria (4 distinct "this worked for me if..." statements)
- Research summary's 9-step learning journey and consolidated design principles
- Audience research Section 5: success indicators by seniority level
- Research summary Section 2.2: consolidated learning sequence and misconception pre-emption
- R2 (Bloom's Tagging): every objective must use an action verb from the taxonomy and be tagged with level

**Design decision:** I structured objectives into 3 tiers matching the brief's scope hierarchy: GA4 Core (primary focus, 7 objectives), GTM Awareness (read/verify level per brief, 2 objectives), and Measurement Strategy & Integration (connecting tools to business outcomes, 3 objectives). Within each tier, objectives progress from lower to higher Bloom's levels following the research summary's prerequisite sequence: conceptual model → reports → event design → explorations → analysis → evaluation.

**Alternative considered:** Organising objectives by day (Day 1 vs Day 2) rather than by topic tier. Rejected because: (a) several objectives span both days (e.g., measurement strategy is introduced on Day 1 and revisited on Day 2), (b) topic-tier organisation makes the prerequisite chain explicit, and (c) module-structure.md handles the day-by-day sequencing separately.

**Trade-off acknowledged:** With 10 hours of net teaching time and a predominantly non-technical audience starting at None/Remember for GA4, I have capped the ceiling at Analyse for most objectives. Evaluate-level is limited to the GA4 configuration audit (which the brief explicitly requires and is scaffolded via checklist). No Create-level objectives are included — building a Looker Studio dashboard from scratch or implementing GTM tags independently would exceed both the time available and the brief's GTM scoping decision. This sacrifices depth for breadth and consolidation time, consistent with R5 (cognitive load management).
