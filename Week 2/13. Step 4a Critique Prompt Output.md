### 3. Weakness Analysis — What is the weakest part?

**Darren's Bloom's Level assignment is internally inconsistent.**

His profile says: GA4: None, GTM: None, WOGAA: Remember — yet his data-driven decision-making is assessed at Understand. But his background explicitly states he "does not produce reports or configure tools — evaluates them." Bloom's Understand at the DDDM level implies he can *explain* concepts, not merely recognise them. However, everything else in his profile (weak intrinsic motivation, "not my job" framing, limited personal analytics experience) suggests his DDDM level should be Remember at most, or Apply only in strategic/non-data contexts.

The inconsistency matters downstream: if curriculum designers take the Understand rating at face value, they will pitch DDDM scaffolding too high for Darren, reducing the friction map's accuracy.

**Fix:** Either lower Darren's DDDM to Remember/Understand with a caveat ("understands data presentations when prepared by others, cannot construct analytical questions independently"), or explain more carefully what "Understand" means in a director-level, non-tool-using context. The current explanation is ambiguous enough to mislead.

### 4. Missing Elements — What would make this significantly better?

Three gaps stand out:

**a) No cross-persona friction interaction map.** The brief mentions mixed seniority as a specific design risk (audience research S4). The output surfaces this in individual personas but never maps how the personas *interact* — e.g., "When Darren is in the room, Siti's passive learner risk intensifies, and Wei Jie's pressure-to-know friction doubles." A 2×2 or simple table of cross-persona dynamics would substantially improve instructional design downstream.

**b) No recommended persona-to-activity mapping.** The output says "these personas drive every design and testing decision downstream" — but it stops before making even provisional connections. A row like "Siti requires explicit relevance bridging by Hour 1 or disengagement likely" would give the curriculum designer an immediate, actionable signal rather than requiring them to re-derive it.

**c) The aspirational flag on Wei Jie is underspecified.** "Mild aspirational risk" is noted but not actioned. What should the curriculum designer do with that? A R7-compliant output would either (a) present a more conservative Wei Jie baseline, or (b) specify a pre-workshop diagnostic to confirm. Flagging without resolving leaves the downstream agent with an unresolved input dependency.
