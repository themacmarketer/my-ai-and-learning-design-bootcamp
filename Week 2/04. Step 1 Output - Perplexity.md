Designing a 2‑day workshop on **Data Analysis with Google Analytics 4 (GA4) and Google Tag Manager (GTM)** for a mixed‑role, non‑coding audience in a government‑tech context is strongly supported by **adult‑learning and evidence‑based instructional‑design research**. Below is a structured, research‑grounded answer divided into five parts:

***

### (1) Research summary: what peer‑reviewed and evidence‑based literature tells us

#### Adult‑learning and mixed‑level cohorts
Research on adult learning highlights five core principles: **self‑direction, prior experience, relevance, problem‑centeredness, and intrinsic motivation** (Knowles’ andragogy; supported by CDC and higher‑ed training guidance). [pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6458658/)
For non‑technical or mixed‑level groups (e.g., branding, marketing, events staff with executives, managers, directors), training is most effective when:
- content starts from **learners’ real work problems** (e.g., “Which event‑page flow leads to more registrations?”), not abstract software features. [pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6388393/)
- facilitators **explicitly acknowledge and build on prior experience** (e.g., “You already make campaign decisions; now we connect those to GA4 data”). [ijsr](https://www.ijsr.net/getabstract.php?paperid=MS2202083303)

#### Workshop duration and dose‑response
Studies on intensive data‑analysis and analytics‑training workshops show that **short, focused sessions (12–20 hours over 2–4 days) with pre‑ and post‑surveys can significantly improve confidence and applied skills**, especially when participants are mixed‑ability. [hdsr.mitpress.mit](https://hdsr.mitpress.mit.edu/pub/0mb0zzlc)
Relevance to participants’ **job roles and policy / service‑delivery goals** strongly predicts long‑term application, which is critical for GovTech Singapore’s public‑sector context. [cepsm](https://cepsm.ca/product/google-analytics-4-ga4-essentials-for-public-sector-and-non-profit-organizations-workshop-june-3-2026/)

#### Hands‑on, problem‑centered, and scaffolded design
Evidence from collaborative data‑analysis workshops and data‑science training for non‑technical researchers shows that:
- **guided, small‑group problem‑solving** on real organizational questions (e.g., “How do citizens complete our service form?”) yields higher engagement and confidence than pure software tours. [academia](https://www.academia.edu/130391679/The_Design_and_Effects_of_Data_Science_Workshops_for_Educational_Researchers)
- learners benefit from **scaffolded complexity**: starting with descriptive metrics (sessions, events, conversions) and moving to segments, filters, and basic customizations, rather than front‑loading implementation or coding. [academia](https://www.academia.edu/130391679/The_Design_and_Effects_of_Data_Science_Workshops_for_Educational_Researchers)

#### Non‑technical audiences and data literacy
Research on training **non‑technical audiences in data‑storytelling and analytics** shows that:
- **modular, bite‑sized topics** (e.g., “Day 1: GA4 basics”; “Day 2: GTM and tagging”) with clear “why → what → how” structures reduce cognitive load and improve retention. [ijsr](https://www.ijsr.net/archive/v11i2/MS2202083303.pdf)
- participants value **templates, checklists, and decision‑trees** (e.g., “What to track for a campaign vs. a service page”) over abstract theory. [cepsm](https://cepsm.ca/product/google-analytics-4-ga4-essentials-for-public-sector-and-non-profit-organizations-workshop-june-3-2026/)

#### Safety in exploration and error‑management
Studies on data‑science and analytics workshops for non‑coders show that:
- **shared, low‑risk environments** (sandbox GA4/GTM accounts, anonymized datasets) reduce anxiety and encourage experimentation. [pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6388393/)
- explicit discussions of **common implementation errors** (e.g., mismatched tags, duplicate tracking, consent compliance) help learners build self‑diagnosis skills. [carnegiehighered](https://www.carnegiehighered.com/google-analytics-4-workshop/)

#### Application to GA4 and GTM design
GA4‑specific and public‑sector training designs (e.g., GA4‑Essentials‑for‑public‑sector workshops) show that:
- workshops should distinguish **GA4 as a decision‑making tool** (not just a dashboard) by linking metrics to **service‑delivery, outreach, and stakeholder‑engagement outcomes**. [cepsm](https://cepsm.ca/product/google-analytics-for-public-sector-and-non-profit-organizations-workshop-june-17-2025/)
- GTM is introduced as a **“no‑code” tracking layer** that reduces dependency on developers, with emphasis on **tags, triggers, events, and basic variables** rather than complex data‑layer coding. [lovesdata](https://www.lovesdata.com/training/google-tag-manager-training/)

### (3) Step‑by‑step “how to” guide for beginners (using Claude Skill)

Assume you are using the **Course‑Building Agent (Claude Skill)** to generate all content, but the pedagogy is grounded in the research above.

#### Step 1: Scope learner profile and objectives
- Use the Skill to generate a **“learner profile”** based on:
  - job roles (branding, marketing, events, managers, directors),  
  - technical level (no coding),  
  - context (GovTech Singapore, public‑service / events‑oriented KPIs).  
- Prompt:  
  > “Draft a learner‑profile table for a 2‑day GA4 + GTM workshop for non‑technical branding, marketing and events staff, including executives, managers, and directors. Include prior experience, expectations, and top‑3 learning outcomes.”

This mirrors **skills‑framework and needs‑analysis practices** used in adult‑education and training design. [skillsfuture.gov](https://www.skillsfuture.gov.sg/docs/default-source/skills-framework/sfw_tae_booklet_20181101_finalv01.pdf)

#### Step 2: Define learning outcomes by level
- Ask the Skill to generate **SMART outcomes** at three levels:
  - Executives: “Interpret GA4 dashboards to evaluate campaign and service‑page performance.”  
  - Managers: “Configure and segment GA4 reports to assess marketing effectiveness.”  
  - Practitioners: “Configure basic GTM tags and events for event and campaign pages.”  

This aligns with **competency‑based adult‑learning frameworks**. [cdc](https://www.cdc.gov/training-development/media/pdfs/2024/04/adult-learning-principles.pdf)

#### Step 3: Structure the 2‑day curriculum
- Prompt the Skill to build a **2‑day agenda** with:
  - **Day 1 – GA4 for decision‑makers and practitioners**  
    - Morning: GA4 fundamentals, metrics, and dashboards (executive‑friendly).  
    - Afternoon: Hands‑on analysis of event / service‑page flows and conversions.  
  - **Day 2 – GTM + advanced GA4**  
    - Morning: GTM basics (tags, triggers, variables) with no‑code examples.  
    - Afternoon: Case‑based workshops where teams design tracking for a real GovTech campaign.  

This mirrors **modular, problem‑centered workshop designs** used in public‑sector analytics training. [hdsr.mitpress.mit](https://hdsr.mitpress.mit.edu/pub/0mb0zzlc)

#### Step 4: Generate bite‑sized modules and activities
- Use the Skill to generate:
  - one‑page “Why → What → How” summaries for each module,  
  - short **“Try this” tasks** (e.g., “Filter users by source/medium to see event‑registration traffic”),  
  - small‑group case studies (e.g., “Design a GA4 event for a form submission”).  

These echo **modular, micro‑learning designs** that improve data literacy for non‑technical audiences. [ijsr](https://www.ijsr.net/getabstract.php?paperid=MS2202083303)

#### Step 5: Build sandbox‑friendly materials
- Ask the Skill to:
  - draft a **“sandbox setup guide”** for participants (separate GA4 property and GTM container for practice).  
  - design **step‑by‑step checklists** for:
    - creating a GA4 property,  
    - configuring basic events and conversions,  
    - deploying a GTM container, and  
    - setting up a simple page‑view or click tag.  

These align with **implementation checklists** used in GA4‑migraton and public‑sector workshops. [cepsm](https://cepsm.ca/product/google-analytics-for-public-sector-and-non-profit-organizations-workshop-june-17-2025/)

#### Step 6: Scaffold from “no code” to “low code”
- Ask the Skill to:
  - start with **GA4 UI navigation, reports, and segments** (no code).  
  - then move to **GTM point‑and‑click workflows** (tags, triggers, variables) with minimal technical jargon.  
  - optionally add **one or two simple data‑layer examples** (e.g., “data‑layer.push for event tracking”) with clear warnings that this is optional.  

This mirrors **low‑code scaffolding** approaches used in GA4/GTM training for marketers. [jellyfish](https://www.jellyfish.com/en-us/training/courses/google-tag-manager)

#### Step 7: Design problem‑centered exercises
- Use the Skill to generate **6–8 scenario‑based exercises**, such as:
  - “Identify which marketing channel drives the most completed registrations.”  
  - “Spot anomalies in event‑page traffic and suggest causes.”  
  - “Configure a GTM tag to track clicks on an ‘Register Now’ button.”  

These are consistent with **collaborative data‑analysis workshops** that focus on real organizational questions. [academia](https://www.academia.edu/130391679/The_Design_and_Effects_of_Data_Science_Workshops_for_Educational_Researchers)

#### Step 8: Embed reflection and feedback
- Ask the Skill to:
  - generate **short reflection prompts** (e.g., “What metric will you start monitoring next week?”).  
  - design a **post‑workshop feedback rubric** (confidence, applicability, and intended next steps).  

These techniques are supported by **evaluation‑based instructional design** that measures actual impact, not just satisfaction. [insurancejournal](https://www.insurancejournal.com/blogs/risk-insurance-education-alliance/2025/11/20/848192.htm)

### (4) Exemplified modules vs. common errors

#### Exemplar Day‑1 structure (GA4)
- **Module 1 – Why GA4 matters for GovTech**  
  - Research‑based: starts from **problem‑centered relevance** (service‑delivery, event‑engagement). [cepsm](https://cepsm.ca/product/google-analytics-4-ga4-essentials-for-public-sector-and-non-profit-organizations-workshop-june-3-2026/)
- **Module 2 – GA4 metrics and dashboards (executive lens)**  
  - Focus on **sessions, users, conversions, and channels** rather than technical setup. [carnegiehighered](https://www.carnegiehighered.com/google-analytics-4-workshop/)
- **Module 3 – Hands‑on analysis of event‑flow funnels**  
  - Use **pre‑built GA4 reports** to walk through a registration funnel. [lovesdata](https://www.lovesdata.com/courses/google-analytics-4/)

#### Exemplar Day‑2 structure (GTM + GA4)
- **Module 4 – GTM as a no‑code tracking layer**  
  - Emphasize **tags, triggers, variables** with screenshot‑style walkthroughs. [lovesdata](https://www.lovesdata.com/training/google-tag-manager-training/)
- **Module 5 – Tagging events for campaigns and forms**  
  - Build **simple ‘click’ events** and **form submission events** without JavaScript deep‑dive. [lovesdata](https://www.lovesdata.com/training/google-tag-manager-training/)
- **Module 6 – Group challenge: design a tracking plan**  
  - Teams propose what to track for a real GovTech campaign (e.g., “Digital Week”), mirroring **collaborative design‑and‑analysis workshops**. [hdsr.mitpress.mit](https://hdsr.mitpress.mit.edu/pub/0mb0zzlc)

#### Common errors to explicitly address
- **Over‑emphasizing implementation detail on Day 1**  
  - Risk: learners disengage if they feel overwhelmed by technical setup.  
  - Fix: use **pre‑configured GA4 sandboxes** and **step‑by‑step checklists**. [carnegiehighered](https://www.carnegiehighered.com/google-analytics-4-workshop/)
- **Ignoring non‑technical role differences**  
  - Risk: executives feel “too simplistic”; practitioners feel “too vague.”  
  - Fix: provide **role‑specific breakout tasks** (e.g., executives interpret dashboards; practitioners configure filters). [cdc](https://www.cdc.gov/training-development/media/pdfs/2024/04/adult-learning-principles.pdf)
- **No explicit error‑diagnosis practice**  
  - Risk: participants struggle when they encounter real‑world tag issues.  
  - Fix: include **“debugging” exercises** (e.g., “Find why this button is not tracked”). [lovesdata](https://www.lovesdata.com/training/google-tag-manager-training/)
